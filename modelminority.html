The "Model" Minority

The Model Minority Myth is a myth that affects Asian Americans and really many Asians in first world countries. It is a stereotype that labels Asians as naturally talented, academically successful, and obedient. This is a myth and a stereotype that can box in Asian youth into a submissive position in the eyes of others. This myth contributes to the usual insults and stereotypical remarks (“Aren’t you good at math?” “Can you play StarCraft?” “I bet you learned like a million instruments” “Of course you’ll do well, you’re Asian”) and over time, can lead Asian youth to believe that if they don’t live up to these impossible standards, they don’t deserve the label of “Asian.”

"Fresh Off The Boat" - ABC

This is a myth that is slowly fading away. With the increase of Asian representation in pop culture and media, the story of Asian youth and Asian Americans is shifting away from the overused narrative of pure academic success and has started to accurately portray how Asian Americans grew up.
